The Importance of Being Earnest

Victorian Societal Norms on Females and the

First Wave of Feminism

Oscar Wilde’s satire not only entertains but also critiques the superficial nature of Victorian society. Through the depiction of female characters, the play reveals the tension between traditional roles and the emerging modernity of women's identity.

Societal Trends

    Gender Expectations: Even empowered women were expected to adhere to the shallow standards of their society.
    Irony and Hypocrisy: Wilde’s witty commentary exposes the contradictions between personal desires and societal expectations.

Female Roles

    Subversion of Conformity: Despite their seemingly conventional roles, these characters subtly assert their agency within restrictive social boundaries.
